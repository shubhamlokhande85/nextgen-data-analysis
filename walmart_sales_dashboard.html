Actionable Business Insights

📦
Front-load holiday inventory
Dec–Nov generates 20%+ of annual revenue. Christmas week alone can spike 250–265% above the mean. Critical SKUs must be stocked by November 15 to prevent stockouts at peak demand.
🏆
Replicate top-store playbook
Store 20 ($301M) outperforms Store 33 ($37M) by 8×. Conduct deep-dive audits of Stores 20, 4, and 14 for merchandising, layout, staffing ratios, and local promotional tactics to codify and scale.
⚠️
Stabilize volatile stores
Stores 35, 7, and 15 have CV >19% — highly erratic revenue. Root-cause analysis needed across local competition, staffing consistency, and regional demand cycles to reduce forecast risk.
📉
Bridge the Jan/Sep slump
January averages $924K, 12% below the annual mean. Targeted post-holiday clearance campaigns, loyalty incentives, and value-tier promotions can recapture $120K–$140K per store in lost weekly revenue.
🏷️
Expand private-label in high-unemployment markets
Unemployment shows the strongest negative correlation (−0.11) with sales. Stores in high-unemployment areas should shift shelf mix toward value brands and private-label SKUs to retain price-sensitive shoppers.
🔍
Fix holiday data quality gap
All top-5 anomaly weeks (Christmas) carry Holiday_Flag = 0, meaning the 7.8% holiday lift figure is understated. Correcting this classification could add 2–3 percentage points to measured holiday impact and improve forecasting accuracy.
